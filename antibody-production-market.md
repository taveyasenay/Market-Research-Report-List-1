<p><p>The antibody production market comprises several types, each with unique characteristics. Polyclonal antibodies are derived from multiple B-cell sources, offering a broad range of recognition, while monoclonal antibodies are produced from a single B-cell clone, providing specificity. Murine antibodies originate from mice, whereas chimeric antibodies combine murine and human components to reduce immunogenicity. Humanized antibodies are predominantly human but retain some murine elements, balancing efficacy and safety in therapeutic applications. Each type serves distinct roles in research and treatment.</p></p>
