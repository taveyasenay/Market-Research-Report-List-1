<p><strong>The Dissolved Oxygen Sensors for Semiconductor Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Polarography Method</li><li>Current Method</li><li>Optical Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Dissolved oxygen sensors for the semiconductor market include three main types: </p><p>1. **Polarography Method**: Utilizes a polarizable electrode to measure oxygen concentration by generating a current proportional to dissolved oxygen levels.</p><p>  </p><p>2. **Current Method**: Measures the electrical current produced by the electrochemical reaction of oxygen at a sensor electrode.</p><p>3. **Optical Method**: Employs luminescent materials that change fluorescence in response to dissolved oxygen, providing a non-invasive measurement with high accuracy. Each method has distinct advantages suited for various semiconductor applications.</p></p>
