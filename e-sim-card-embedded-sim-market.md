<p><p>An E-SIM (Embedded SIM) is a programmable SIM card integrated directly into devices, replacing traditional physical SIM cards. It offers advantages in space, durability, and connectivity. The E-SIM market consists of two primary types: SMD (Surface Mount Device) which involves soldering the E-SIM onto circuit boards for devices like smartphones and wearables, and SIP (System in Package), which includes integrated circuits in a compact module for IoT applications. Both types enhance device design flexibility and ease of deployment.</p></p>
