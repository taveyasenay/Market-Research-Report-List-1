<p><p>Self-lubricated bearings are engineered components designed to operate without the need for external lubrication. They incorporate self-lubricating materials, often composites or polymers, that release a small amount of lubricant during operation, reducing friction and wear. This unique feature makes them ideal for applications where regular maintenance is challenging, such as in enclosed or hard-to-reach locations. <p><p>The self-lubricated bearing market is categorized into two main types: metallic and non-metallic self-lubricated bearings. Metallic self-lubricated bearings typically comprise materials like bronze or steel, providing strength and durability while utilizing embedded lubricants for prolonged operation. In contrast, non-metallic self-lubricated bearings, often made from polymers or composite materials, offer lightweight options with excellent resistance to corrosion and wear. Both types are designed to minimize maintenance needs and enhance performance in various applications across industries.</p></p>
